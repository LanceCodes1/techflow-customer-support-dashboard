# Case Study: Improving Customer Support Wait Times at TechFlow Solutions
## Business Issue: The Long Wait Time Problem

### Company
TechFlow Solutions (a fictional mid-sized internet service provider)

### Problem
Customers are experiencing excessively long wait times when calling customer support. Management suspects that staffing levels don't match call volume patterns, but they don't have historical data to analyze the problem.

### Customer Impact Example
Sarah, a small business owner, loses her internet connection during peak business hours. She calls TechFlow support at 2:15 PM and waits 42 minutes before reaching an agent. During this time, her team can't work, and she loses potential sales. Frustrated, she tweets about her negative experience, damaging TechFlow's reputation.

### Root Cause Hypothesis:
Support managers suspect that certain days of the week and times of day have much higher call volumes, but they haven't tracked this systematically. Without data, they can't justify hiring more staff or adjusting schedules.

## Project Solution: Data-Driven Staffing Analysis
### Objective
Create a simulated dataset of customer support calls to visualize patterns in call volume and wait times, then use this analysis to recommend optimal staffing schedules.

### Tools for Beginner Programmers:
- Python with `Faker` library: To generate realistic fake call data
- `pandas`: For data organization
- Python's `random` library: For number methods
- `datetime`: For time values
- Lovable (plotting library): To create clear visualizations showing pattern

## Required Data
You need to effectively capture customer information, so you will require the following pieces of information:
### Customer
* A unique ID per each customer (UUID generated by `faker`)
* Customer names (String generated by `faker`)
* Timestamp for the customer call (Date generated by `datetime`)
* Day of the week that the call took place (String, determined by the timestamp of the call)
* Hour of the day the call took place (Date - Day format, determined by the timestamp of the call)
* Wait time in minutes (Integer, generated by `random`)
* Issue category: `Billing`, `Technical`, `Service`, `Account` (Array index, determined by `random`)
* Resolution status (Boolean, determined by `random`)

## How to Use the Data
1. Create a function that does the following:
    1. Create a way to store `call_records`
    2. Generates at least 1000 records *iteratively* to store in `call_records`
        1. In doing so, the function should generate the timestamp for the customer call using a `Faker` generator, day of the week that the call took place based on the call's timestamp in day format, `hour` of the day that the call took place based on the call's timestamp
        2. Check if the call happened during work hours (9-5), based on a 24-hour day, using the above `hour` of the day. If so, generate a wait time between `10` and `45` minutes. Otherwise, generate a wait time between `2` and `15` minutes
        3. Check if the day of the week is `'Monday'` or `'Friday'` and increase wait time by a random integer (using Numpy's `random` method) between `5` and `15` minutes to emulate peak call times on the busiest days
        4. Once all this is evaluated, the `call_records` should add new pieces of data including call id, customer name, call timestamp, data of the week, hour of the day, wait time in minutes, issue category, and resolution status
2. Create a dataframe with `Pandas` to create a basic representation of the `call_records` table
3. Use `pandas` to export a `.csv` file to be used in Lovable in the next section
4. Resolve any errors arising in the terminal as you develop your project

## Business Recommendations Based on Data
**Key Analysis Questions for Students**:
- Which day has the longest average wait times?
- What are the 3 busiest hours of the day?
- How would you adjust staffing based on these patterns?
